Why a team of specialists instead of one do-everything AI?
The same reason a kitchen has both a prep cook and a head chef: a specialist with one clear job is cheaper, more reliable, and safer. For example, only the Poster can touch the "publish" button — so nothing can ever go out by accident.
In tech terms: each staff member is a LangGraph node. The "only the Poster can publish" rule is called least-privilege tools.

Two hidden helpers behind the crew

Two things make the crew actually work day to day. They're easy to confuse, so here's each in one line:

🧠 The "brain" inside each staffer — LangGraph + Claude

This is the real thinking: writing a caption, judging whether a DM is risky, retrying when something comes out wrong. It's the skill each team member has.

🛎️ The "office assistant" — n8n

This one doesn't think. It watches the clock ("6pm — time to post"), answers the doorbell ("a new comment just arrived"), and taps you on the shoulder ("approve this one?"). It runs errands and carries messages.

"If we have the assistant, do we still need the brain?"
Yes. The assistant can ring a bell and fetch you, but it can't write a caption or read the mood of an angry DM. The brain is brilliant at the craft, but it can't watch a clock or text your phone. You need both: the assistant handles timing and messages; the brain handles thinking.

✅ The Checker — the brand guardian

Before anything reaches you, the Checker confirms it truly sounds like Voodoo Momo and breaks none of your rules.

Think of a strict proofreader who knows your brand book by heart. If the Maker gets carried away and writes "BEST MOMOS IN PUNE!!! 🔥🔥", the Checker stops it at the door.

What it checks, every single time

  • Between 8 and 12 hashtags — and your 3 must-have ones are present
  • No banned words ("best momos", "you deserve", over-the-top hype)
  • Caption not too long, and only your approved emojis

The "how sure are you?" score. Every staffer also rates its own confidence, from 0 to 100%. If the Maker is only, say, 50% sure, the post is automatically held back for you — it can't slip through. It's like a junior cook saying, "Chef, can you taste this? I'm not certain."

Why bother? AI sometimes drifts off-brand. The Checker is the safety net that guarantees whatever the AI writes is either on-brand — or flagged for you. working now

💬 The Host — talks to your customers

Once a post is live, people comment and send DMs. The Host handles them — and knows exactly when to call you in.

Think of a warm, friendly person at the counter. They happily answer "what time do you close?" on their own — but if a customer is upset, asks about catering for 40, or mentions a refund, they instantly say, "Let me get the manager."

Is it ready for real, everyday use?

Not yet — but it works. Think of it as a kitchen that can cook one dish beautifully, but isn't a fully running restaurant yet. You can create and publish a great post today; it just needs more before it can run on its own, every day.
