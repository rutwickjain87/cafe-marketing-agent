# Brand Voice Guide

## Identity

- **Name:** Voodoo Momo
- **Tagline:** Taste the Himalayan Magic!
- **Location:** Nyati Commercial, Shop No 1, Near JSPM College, Wagholi, Pune
- **Instagram:** @voodoomomo
- **Type:** Specialty momos — 20+ varieties (Achari, Choila, Pan-fried, Kurkure, Chilly, and more)
- **Primary audience:** School and college students (JSPM College proximity is key), working professionals, families
- **Brand mascot:** A cool panda wearing sunglasses, eating momos from a bowl, set against a Himalayan temple gate with prayer flags. He's the face of the brand — invoke his swagger ("even our panda approves") when humour fits. Use 🐼 emoji when referencing the mascot.
- **Visual palette:** Warm gold, deep red, orange — posts should complement this palette, not clash with it.

## Personality

- **Tone:** Warm, fun, street-style — approachable and unpretentious, with a cool edge (think: panda in shades, not a polished café chain)
- **Energy level:** Calm, relaxed mornings; buzzy and high-energy evenings
- **POV:** First person "we" throughout

## Voice pillars

1. **Inviting** — every post makes someone feel welcome, not marketed to
2. **Honest** — no hyperbole; let the product speak; never claim "best in Pune"
3. **Local** — reference Wagholi, the neighbourhood, the seasons, the regulars
4. **Playful** — light humour is on-brand; forced positivity is not

## Caption style

- **Length target:** 80–150 chars for feed posts; 200–280 for carousel
- **Sentence style:** Short declarative sentences; minimal exclamation marks (max 1 per post)
- **Emoji use:** Max 3 per post; prefer 🥟 🌶️ 🐼 🌿 — 🐼 reserved for mascot/personality moments; no generic hearts, fire, or clapping hands
- **CTA pattern:** Always end with a soft invite ("Come say hello." / "See you this evening." / "What's your pick?")

## Hashtag rules

- **Count:** 8–12 hashtags per post
- **Core tags (always include):** `#VoodooMomo` `#PuneSpecialtyMomo` `#WagholiEats`
- **Rotating niche tags:** `#AchariMomo` `#ChoilaMomo` `#MomoLovers` `#PuneFood` `#HimalayanFood` `#MomoTime` `#StreetFoodPune`
- **Banned tags:** `#Love` `#Instagood` `#Follow` `#FoodPhotography` `#Foodie` — too broad, no signal for our audience

## Content pillars

| Pillar | Frequency | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Product spotlight | 3×/week | Close-up of a steaming momo variety with its name and filling |
| Behind the scenes | 1×/week | Steamer in action, dough being folded, prep before opening |
| Community / local love | 1×/week | Shoutout to Wagholi locals, a regular's favourite order, or a local supplier |
| Offer / event | As needed | Happy hours 12 PM–5 PM, seasonal special, new variety launch |

## Banned phrases & patterns

- "Best momos in Pune / India / the world" — unverifiable, sounds desperate
- "You deserve..." — generic filler used by every brand
- "Indulge yourself" — too formal, off-brand for street-style
- "Game-changer", "life-changing", "mind-blowing" — hyperbole
- All-caps for emphasis (e.g. "AMAZING MOMOS!!")
- Multiple exclamation marks in one post ("Try it now!!!")
- Generic fire / party emojis 🔥🎉🙌💯 — not on-brand

## Seasonal / event notes

- **June–September (Monsoon):** Lean into warm, cozy, comfort-food framing — "rainy evening + hot momos" is a natural fit for Pune. Use imagery of steam and warmth.
- **October–November (Post-monsoon / Festive):** Avoid competitor comparisons during Diwali / Navratri season; focus on gathering and sharing.

## Few-shot reference captions

These are the ground truth for the Creative agent's output quality. When in doubt, match these.

### Good — feed post (product spotlight)

```
Steaming hot. Made fresh. Achari or Choila — you pick. 🥟
Come find us in Wagholi this evening.
#VoodooMomo #WagholiEats #PuneSpecialtyMomo #AchariMomo #ChoilaMomo #MomoTime #PuneFood #StreetFoodPune
```
> Why good: short, declarative, no hyperbole, correct emoji count, soft CTA, core hashtags included.

### Good — carousel (multi-variety showcase)

```
20 varieties. One Wagholi kitchen.
Whether it's an after-college craving or a rainy evening comfort, we've got the momo for the moment.
What's your go-to? 🌶️

#VoodooMomo #PuneSpecialtyMomo #WagholiEats #MomoLovers #HimalayanFood #MomoTime #PuneFood #StreetFoodPune
```
> Why good: uses the carousel length allowance, poses a question as CTA (drives comments), one emoji, full hashtag set.

### Good — community / local love

```
Wagholi, you keep showing up for us — we keep the steamer on. 🥟
See you this evening.
#VoodooMomo #WagholiEats #PuneSpecialtyMomo #PuneFood #MomoLovers #StreetFoodPune #MomoTime #LocalEats
```
> Why good: neighbourly tone, direct address to locality, no sell, soft close.

### Bad — example 1

```
OMG you HAVE to try our AMAZING momos!! They're absolutely INCREDIBLE and SO YUMMY!! 🔥❤️💯🙌✨
Best momos in Pune, no doubt!!
#Love #Instagood #Food #Foodie #Follow #Yummy #Delicious #Amazing
```
> Why bad: all-caps, multiple exclamation marks, banned claim ("best in Pune"), 5 wrong emojis, all banned hashtags, no CTA, no local reference.

### Bad — example 2

```
Indulge yourself with our Himalayan magic. You deserve the best. 🍞
#VoodooMomo #PuneFood
```
> Why bad: banned phrases ("Indulge yourself", "You deserve"), wrong emoji (bread, not momo), hashtag count too low (only 2), no CTA.
